TF-IDF in veck.js
Intro

Let's supposed we'd like to build a search engine for a bunch of documents. We'd like to be able to submit a query, a series of words and have it return documents which have those words inside them. How would we do this? Well, initially you might think, we can jjust match all the documents that have words that match the words in our query. Then, we can sort them by how often those terms appear. If you thought that, you're on the right track.

This is the basis of the first half of our TF-IDF, i.e. TF, term-frequency. Simply put, what we're going to do is represent our documents as vectors of terms and their frequencies. Then, we're going to match the terms in our queries to that in the documents and we shall sort them by their frequencies.

We will now use a bit of JS to make make a simple bag of words and represent all these as vectors.

Cool, now we have vector representations of term frequencies accross all the documents. If you are lost, here's a simple way to explain things.

suppose, we represent the words, 'the','and','he' in an vector. It'd look like this:

['the','and','he']

     Now, each document has a specific amount of these words, so the vector representation for each document, is that amount. i.e. if Document 1 has 4 the's, 2 and's and 5 he's. It will have a vector representation like this.

[4, 2, 5]

     This is the bag of words model. That is what the above script did, only for all the words in the entire document set.
